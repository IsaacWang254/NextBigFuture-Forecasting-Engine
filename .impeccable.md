## Design Context

### Users
NBF Forecasting Engine users are technical forecasters, deep-tech investors, operators, and researchers scanning many technology domains at once. They need to see current model predictions, evidence, uncertainty, and category-level movement quickly enough to decide what deserves deeper analysis.

### Brand Personality
Analytical, decisive, frontier-aware. The product should feel like a serious forecasting desk for future technology rather than a marketing page or a generic analytics dashboard.

### Aesthetic Direction
Industrial editorial console: light, dense, readable, and evidence-forward. Use restrained neutrals with strategic signal colors for probability and movement. Avoid flashy neon AI aesthetics, oversized hero sections, vague gradients, and decorative charts that do not carry information.

### Design Principles
- Put forecasts and evidence in the first viewport; do not hide the product behind landing-page copy.
- Favor dense, scannable layouts with clear hierarchy over decorative card grids.
- Make uncertainty visible through probabilities, confidence, horizon, and caveats.
- Keep controls familiar: tabs for categories, progress indicators for probabilities, compact tables for ranked predictions.
- Use concise, high-signal language; every label should help interpretation or action.
